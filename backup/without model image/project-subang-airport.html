Subang Airport Regeneration Plan

Overview of Subang Airport Regeneration Plan:

The Subang Airport Regeneration Plan is a transformative project in Selangor, Malaysia, aimed at revitalizing the airport and its surrounding infrastructure. It includes upgrading utility systems and infrastructure to support expanded aviation and commercial activities.

Project Details

  • Location: Selangor, Malaysia
  • Duration: Feb 2024 - June 2024
  • LOD: 300
  • Role: Senior BIM Modeler
  • Worked Firm: HSS BIM Solutions Pvt. Ltd.
  • Tools Used: Revit, Civil 3D, Navisworks, AutoCAD
  • Reference: Project Reference

Key Contributions

  • Modelled sewer, drainage, telecom, water, low- and high-voltage electrical services, and streetlights in Civil 3D.
  • Modelled road furniture in Revit, including medians, lanes, and signage.
  • Conducted clash detection using Navisworks to ensure a clash-free model.

Challenges and Solutions

Modeling a wide range of utilities, including sewer, drainage, telecom, water, and electrical services, alongside road furniture in a complex airport environment presented challenges due to the need for precise integration with existing infrastructure. The high-traffic setting required careful coordination to avoid conflicts with other disciplines.

Outcomes

Delivered a clash-free BIM model encompassing utilities and road furniture, supporting the Subang Airport Regeneration Plan’s infrastructure goals. Ensuring reliable deliverables that contributed to the project’s efficiency and successful planning for future construction phases.
